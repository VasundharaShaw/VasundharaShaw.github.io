About me

Dr Vasundhara Shaw

Astrophysicist & Research Software Engineer

FIZ Karlsruhe – Leibniz Institute for Information Infrastructure, Berlin

Hiya — I go by Vasu. I'm an astrophysicist and research software engineer at FIZ Karlsruhe – Leibniz Institute for Information Infrastructure in Berlin, where my work bridges scientific research and open research infrastructure. My astrophysics research focuses on the Galactic magnetic field (GMF), ultra-high energy cosmic rays (UHECRs), and polarised synchrotron emission using data from the C‑BASS and S‑PASS surveys.

On the software engineering side, I contribute to Jupyter4NFDI — part of Germany's National Research Data Infrastructure (NFDI) — building cloud-based, reproducible research environments that make computational science more accessible across disciplines. This means working with JupyterHub deployments, containerisation, and FAIR data practices alongside a team of researchers and RSEs.

I also build numerical simulations with CRPropa3 to track how cosmic rays propagate through the magnetised Galactic halo. I completed my PhD at DESY Zeuthen before moving to the Jodrell Bank Centre for Astrophysics in Manchester and then to Berlin. Beyond research, I'm on the steering committee of Astronomers for Planet Earth and care about equity and sustainability in science.

What I work on

Research Interests

My work connects observations, simulations, and theory across three main themes.

Galactic Magnetic Field Modelling

I compare analytic GMF models against full-sky 5 GHz synchrotron data from C-BASS and S-PASS, using T-T plots and template-fitting frameworks I have developed. This constrains the three-dimensional structure of both the disk and halo magnetic field.

C-BASS (5 GHz)S-PASS (2.3 GHz)Rotation measure mapsTemplate fitting

Ultra-High Energy Cosmic Ray Propagation

I simulate UHECR transport through the Galactic halo using CRPropa3, studying how isotropic turbulent magnetic fields of varying strengths deflect particles and shape the observed dipole and quadrupole anisotropy pattern on the sky.

CRPropa3Pierre Auger ObservatoryMultipole analysisFermi/eROSITA bubbles

Radio Polarimetry & Survey Science

I contribute to C-BASS data analysis (polarisation morphology, QU pipeline) and to POSSUM — the Polarisation Sky Survey of the Universe's Magnetism conducted with ASKAP — probing the magnetised interstellar medium across the southern sky.
