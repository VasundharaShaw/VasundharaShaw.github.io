About me

Dr Vasundhara Shaw

Hiya — I go by Vasu. I'm an astrophysicist and research software engineer at FIZ Karlsruhe – Leibniz Institute for Information Infrastructure in Berlin, where my work bridges scientific research and open research infrastructure.

My astrophysics research focuses on the Galactic magnetic field (GMF), ultra-high energy cosmic rays (UHECRs), and polarised synchrotron emission using data from the C‑BASS and S‑PASS surveys. I build numerical simulations with CRPropa3 to track how cosmic rays propagate through the magnetised Galactic halo.

On the software side, I contribute to Jupyter4NFDI — part of Germany's National Research Data Infrastructure — building cloud-based, reproducible research environments with JupyterHub, Binder, and repo2docker. I completed my PhD at DESY Zeuthen, then moved to the Jodrell Bank Centre for Astrophysics in Manchester, and now Berlin.

Beyond research, I'm on the steering committee of Astronomers for Planet Earth and care deeply about equity and sustainability in science.

What I work on

Research Interests

Galactic Magnetic Field Modelling

I compare analytic GMF models against full-sky 5 GHz synchrotron data from C-BASS and S-PASS, using T-T plots and template-fitting frameworks to constrain the 3D structure of disk and halo fields.

C-BASS 5 GHzS-PASS 2.3 GHzT-T plotsTemplate fitting

UHECR Propagation

I simulate UHECR transport through the Galactic halo using CRPropa3, studying how turbulent magnetic fields deflect particles and shape the observed dipole and quadrupole anisotropy pattern.

CRPropa3Pierre AugerMultipole analysisFermi bubbles

Radio Polarimetry & Surveys

I contribute to C-BASS polarisation analysis (QU pipeline, morphology) and to POSSUM — the ASKAP polarisation sky survey — probing the magnetised ISM across the southern sky.

Open Science Infrastructure

Through Jupyter4NFDI I contribute to reproducible, cloud-based research workflows for the German academic community, making computational astrophysics more accessible and FAIR.
